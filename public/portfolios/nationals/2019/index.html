Reedʼs Cheese Food Truck was founded on the ideal of spreading comfort to people in a tangible way. As a child, Reed was terrified of storms. As soon as the sky darkened, he would crawl under his bed and stay there for hours. Growing up in the Midwest, this made the Spring and Summer months very difficult for him. During a particularly long storm, Reedʼs mother managed to coax him out from under his bed and to the kitchen table with the promise of his favorite sandwich, grilled cheese. Since it worked once, it quickly became a ritual for them. Soon, storms became a special time of comfort and closeness for Reed and his mother, always accompanied by grilled cheese sandwiches.

The idea of spreading this comfort to people came to Reed as a highschooler and followed him through college and into his career. It took his cousin Brent, though, to come up with the idea of the food truck. They went into business together and so Reedʼs Cheese Food Truck was born. Nowadays you can find Reed and Brent all over the city, at various events, and parks. They are one of the few food trucks that will stay open during a storm, serving up grilled cheese sandwiches just like Reedʼs mom used to make.
